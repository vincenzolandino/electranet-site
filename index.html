Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to the most common questions Connecticut homeowners and businesses ask about electrical services.

The cost of an electrical panel upgrade in Connecticut typically ranges from $2,500 to $7,000 depending on the amperage (100A, 200A, or 400A), the condition of your existing wiring, and whether permits are required. Electranet provides free estimates for all panel upgrade projects. Call 203.314.9928 for a personalized quote.

Yes, Connecticut law requires that electrical work be performed by a licensed electrician. Electranet holds a State of Connecticut E-1 electrical license with unlimited scope, meaning we are authorized to perform any type of electrical work (residential, commercial, or industrial) throughout the state.

Common signs your electrical panel needs an upgrade include: circuit breakers that trip frequently, flickering or dimming lights, a fuse box instead of a breaker panel, burning smell near the panel, you're planning to add major appliances or an EV charger, or your home still has 60-amp or 100-amp service. We provide free electrical inspections to assess your panel.

Yes! Electranet installs Level 2 EV chargers for all electric vehicle brands including Tesla Wall Connector, ChargePoint, and universal J1772 chargers. We handle everything from panel assessment to permitting to final installation. Many CT homes require a panel upgrade to support the 240V circuit needed for Level 2 charging.

Whole house standby generator installation in Connecticut typically costs between $5,000 and $15,000 depending on the generator size, fuel type (natural gas or propane), transfer switch requirements, and installation complexity. Electranet provides free on-site consultations to determine the right generator size for your home.

In Connecticut, an E-1 license is an unlimited electrical contractor license, allowing the holder to perform any type of electrical work with no restrictions on project size or complexity. An E-2 license is a limited license with restrictions. Electranet holds the E-1 license, meaning we can handle everything from residential ceiling fans to full industrial facility wiring.

Knob-and-tube wiring, common in Connecticut homes built before 1950, can be a fire hazard, especially when covered by insulation or when circuits are overloaded by modern appliances. We recommend having it inspected by a licensed electrician. Electranet specializes in whole-house rewiring to replace outdated knob-and-tube and aluminum wiring.

Yes! Electranet provides free estimates for all electrical projects: residential, commercial, and industrial. Call us at 203.314.9928 or fill out the contact form on this page to schedule your free consultation. We serve all of Connecticut with a focus on the greater New Haven area.
